Task Streams

Task streams are the longest uninterrupted chains of No Blocker and Soft Blocker steps. These chains become the foundation for prompt creation — they represent work a Portfolio Manager can do end-to-end with at most minor data pre-fetching.

Task Stream A — Primary

Signal Construction → Investment Committee Presentation

Steps 1.1 → 5.1

The full multi-strategy analytical pipeline from universe definition through thesis development and Investment Committee memorandum preparation. Covers dispersion signal construction, volatility harvesting screening, directional volatility signals, model calibration, multi-strategy backtesting, peer review, portfolio design for all five strategy legs, scenario analysis, and attribution methodology — the complete intellectual arc of building an integrated volatility portfolio.

30 steps
25 No Blocker · 5 Soft Blocker · Terminates at 5.2 (Investment Committee Vote — Hard Blocker)

Post-Trade Monitoring → Rebalance Approval

Master Portfolio Workflow

End-to-End Canonical Portfolio Management stages: Idea Generation & Sourcing → Research Aggregation → Collaboration & Peer Review → Thesis Development → Decision, Approval, & Portfolio Construction → Monitoring & Review → Rebalancing & Liquidity

Complete 7-stage process from multi-strategy signal construction through portfolio monitoring and rebalancing. Five strategy legs — dispersion, volatility harvesting, directional long/short, dynamic volatility targeting, and option overlays — flow through all gates, feedback loops, and output documents. Task Stream A (steps 1.1–5.1, 30 steps) covers the full analytical pipeline.
